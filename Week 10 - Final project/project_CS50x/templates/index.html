Introduction

This website will compare the 30 most commonly eaten options in the specific food group known as “Meats”, based on 5 nutrient quantity: Calories, Protein, Fats, Saturated fats (SF), Monounsaturated fats (MUF), Polyunsaturated fats (PUF).

To compare up to 5 different meat options, click the Compare tab above.

What are Meats?

Meat, for animal products, is often the most significant part of many people's diets and are often the centrepiece in any given meal.

What makes meats different from other food types is that they are the primary source of protein in most diets as they are known as a complete protein (or ideal or high-quality protein), meaning they contain all nine essential amino acids.

To see the data on all 30 meat options, click the Tabulated Data menu above.

Why choose?

There are many different meat options available, so why choose when one can eat whatever's convenient or even eat them all?

Well, meats are eaten based on different intentions, for example, weight loss or lean muscle growth, developing muscular endurance, bulking, cutting or muscular maintenance.

Thus, it is important to be selective with your meats to ensure you are consuming the right type of meat suitable for your personal health reasons as well as negating possible negative health effects of the different meats.

To learn more about proteins and amino acids, click the Protein menu above.

Disclaimer

This website only lists the different meat options based on 5 different factors.

Therefore, one should not assume one meat is better than another or make significant dietary decisions based on the information in this website before further research.

Each of the meats in this list has their own nutritional value and they contain a wide variety of different vitamins, antioxidants, minerals and acids of varying levels. Thus, each meat option has its own advantages and disadvantages and meet different nutritional needs.
