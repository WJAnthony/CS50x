The 6 key nutrients

As mentioned, the 30 meats featured in this website have some of their nutrientional information included. This includes 6 important nutrients: Calories, Protein, Fats, Saturated fats (SF), Monounsaturated fats (MUF), Polyunsaturated fats (PUF).

These 6 nutrients were selected as information on them are the most commonly searched for and they represent the most significant nutriental factors when it comes to choosing a meat.

If you have read the protein page, you probably know a lot more about proteins, but do you know the difference between saturated and unsaturated fats? Fret not, scroll down to learn more about the 6 nutrients!

Nutrients 101

Protein

The more, the better

  • Protein is a nutrient our body needs to grow and repair cells, crucial for muscle building

Learn more

Calories

Daily recommended caloric intake is 2500 kcal for men and 2000 kcal for women

  • Calories measure the amount of energy in food we consume
  • Your weight will increase if you consume more calories than you burn as your body stores the extra energy as fat
  • Your weight will decrease if you consume less calories than you burn as your body uses its stored fat for energy

Learn more

Fats

More 'good' fats, less 'bad' fats

  • A small amount of fat is essential for a healthy, balanced diet as a source of essential fatty acids, which our body cannot produce.
  • Fats aid the absorbtion of vitamins and are stored caloric dense source of energy our body utilises to function
  • Unhealthy fats include saturated fats and trans fats
  • Healthy fats include monounsaturated fats and polyunsaturated fats

Learn more

Saturated Fats

An unhealthy fat, together with trans fats

  • Consuming too much saturated fats can raise the levels "bad" low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol in your blood
  • Fats aid the absorbtion of vitamins and are stored caloric dense source of energy our body utilises to function
  • Excessive intake of saturated fats increases the risk of heart disease, strokes and weight gain
  • Avoid, limit or replace foods in your diet that are high in saturated fats, such as butter, cream or ice cream

Learn more

Monounsaturated Fats (MUF)

A healthy fat

  • Monounsaturated fats are good for heart health as it helps maintain levels of "good" HDL cholesterol while reducing levels of "bad" LDL cholesterol in the blood
  • Foods that are naturally high in MUF: Olive & Rapeseed oil, Avocados, Almonds, Peanuts and Brazilian nuts

Learn more

Polyunsaturated Fats (PUF)

A healthy fat

  • Polyunsaturated fats can help lower the level of "bad" LDL cholesterol in the blood
  • 2 main types of PUF: Omega-3 and Omega-6
  • Foods naturally high in Omega-6: Vegetable oils (e.g. sunflower and corn) and Nuts(e.g. almonds, cashews, walnuts)
  • Foods naturally high in Omega-3: Oily fish such as sardines, salmons and mackerel
