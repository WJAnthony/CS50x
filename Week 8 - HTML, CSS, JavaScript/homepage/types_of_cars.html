Different car types

There are hundred of different models for sale today and many car types featured. Although some car types have become much less common, there are 9 major categories of cars. The 9 categories each have their own unique uses and characteristics.

Far and away the most popular body style, SUVs are renowned for their versatility. They tend to be taller and boxier than sedans and have a higher ground clearance. Originally truck-based, SUVS were built primarily to traverse rough terrain as well as carry or tow heavy loads Body-on-frame SUVs still exist, but the most common SUVs on the road today have many modern features and offer superior on-road comfort

Before crossover SUVs became popular, the four-door sedan was the predominant vehicle type. Sedans feature a distinct three-box designs, engine in front, cabin in the center, and trunk in the rear. They are still the most common type of cars on the road today but SUVs are catching up.

The most popular car type solded in the United States. Historically pickup trucks were designed as commerical vehicles that could carry workers and bring all equipment in an open bed behind the cab. These pickups became a symbol of hard work and practicality, adding to their desirability. Modern trucks feature beeter efficiency and comfort compared to their predecessors.

While four-door sedans were the practical family option, two-door coupes were the sportier, fashion-forward option. Coupes are often prettier but less practical and commonly feature in performance cars. For examole, muscle cars, sports cars, hypercars, and grand tourers all have two doors and are rarely seen with layouts other than as coupes.

An Aston Martin Vantage

Sports cars are styled and built to bring joy to drivers and onlookers. They are the sportiest, hottest, coolest-looking coupes. Sports cars are low to the ground, sleek, and often expensive and are generally two-seaters.

A Porsche 911 GT3RS

Wagons are based on sedans but have an extended roofline and a hatch door at the rear instead of a trunk. This allows for a larger cargo area so drivers can carry more. Wagons' popularity have declined in recent years but remain popular in europe.

A Volvo v90

Stereotypically, hatchbacks, or more commonly known as hot hatches, are cheap, fun to drive, practical, and small. Traditionally, hatchback had meant a compact sedan with a more squary shaped and a rear flip-up hatch door instead of a conventional trunk as access to the car's cargo area. Due to their shape, hatchbacks tend to have more legroom for passengers in the second row but less cargo space when compare to sedans.

A Volkswagon Golf Type R

Convertibles allows you to drop the top, stowing the roof for wind-in-the-hair bliss. Some convertibles use a fabric or vinyl roof while others utilise a folding hard-top design.

A Mazda MX5

Vans prioritise practicality over style and were orginally commerical vehicles. They have a large rectagular boxy shape and now come in many different forms, ranging from minivans carrying many passengers to large cargo haulers.
